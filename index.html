Nanis Kanana is an environmental, social and occupational safety leader with 10 years' experience across diverse sectors including construction, manufacturing, energy, and logistics. She is the founder of Sustainability Pulse Africa (SPAL), a company providing comprehensive OSH solutions, environmental impact studies & audits, sustainability reporting, carbon emissions accounting, resource efficiency, and E&S due diligence services, alongside the provision of impact reporting digital platforms.

Nanis is passionate about helping organizations ensure safety and sustainability of their operations. She is award winner of SOWSHEA Africa sustainability leadership award. She has worked with companies across East, West and Central Africa to ensure integration of social and environmental considerations in management systems and decision making. Some of the organizations include Sun King (Africa & Asia), PowerGen Renewable energy, Harj construction limited, D.M Bchanga construction limited, EMI Africa, UK Institution of Occupational Safety & Health, EiSqr Asia.

She holds a Masters in Occupational Safety & Health and a Bachelors in Environmental Science from Kenyatta University. She is certified in carbon accounting for renewable energy projects, Sustainability Global Reporting Initiative, NEBOSH IGC, and ISO 9001:2015 internal auditing. Nanis area of expertise is environmental and social due diligence, audits, trainings, carbon accounting, occupational safety and health management and sustainability reporting. In addition, she has experience driving strategic relationships and collaborations with key stakeholders. Nanis is the East Africa IOSH consultant she drives strategic government and organizational partnerships to improve OSH in the landscape.

She is an Executive Committee Member of Workplace Safety Professional association of Kenya. The largest safety professional association spearheading high standard safety management performance within the country. She is also the country representative for Africa Safety Award for Excellence (AfriSAFE) and an associate member of the National Environmental and Coordination Authority (NEMA). She is also a member of GHG Institute and Sustainability Champions Network.
